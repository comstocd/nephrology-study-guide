28. Calcium Disorders

Hypercalcemia

Most common: Hyperparathyroidism (outpatient), Malignancy (inpatient)

Symptoms

"Stones, bones, groans, moans, psychiatric overtones"

Workup

  • PTH high: Primary hyperparathyroidism
  • PTH low: Malignancy, granulomatous, vitamin D toxicity

Treatment

  1. IV NS (volume first)
  2. Loop diuretics (after euvolemic)
  3. Bisphosphonates (zoledronic acid)
  4. Calcitonin (rapid but tachyphylaxis)
  5. Dialysis (severe)

Hypocalcemia

  • Causes: Hypoparathyroid (post-surgical), vitamin D deficiency, CKD, hypomagnesemia
  • Signs: Chvostek (facial twitch), Trousseau (carpal spasm)
  • Treatment: IV calcium gluconate if symptomatic

44. Board-Style Practice Questions

Q1. AKI Classification

65M with CHF presents with Cr 2.4 (baseline 1.0). BUN 56. UNa 8, FENa 0.4%. Bland sediment. Most likely?

Answer: Prerenal AKI (low FENa, high BUN:Cr, bland sediment)

Q2. AIN

45F started on omeprazole 3 weeks ago. Now Cr 2.8, fever, rash, eosinophilia. UA shows WBC casts. Next step?

Answer: Stop PPI. If no improvement in 5-7 days, consider steroids.

Q3. Glomerulonephritis

22M with gross hematuria during URI. Normal C3. UA shows RBC casts. Most likely diagnosis?

Answer: IgA nephropathy (synpharyngitic hematuria, normal complement)

Q4. Nephrotic Syndrome

60M with new nephrotic syndrome, anti-PLA2R positive. What screening is essential?

Answer: Age-appropriate cancer screening (membranous + age >50 = rule out malignancy)

Q5. RTA

55F with diabetes on lisinopril. K 6.2, HCO3 18, pH 7.30. Urine pH 5.2. Which RTA?

Answer: Type 4 RTA (hyperkalemia, acidosis, low urine pH, on ACEi)

Q6. Hyponatremia

68F with SCLC. Na 118, Sosm 260, Uosm 450, UNa 45. Euvolemic. Treatment?

Answer: SIADH from SCLC. Fluid restriction first-line. Correct â‰¤8-10 mEq/24h.

Q7. Hyperkalemia

K 7.2 with peaked T waves on ECG. First treatment?

Answer: IV calcium gluconate (stabilizes myocardium)

Q8. TMA

35F with MAHA, plt 25K, Cr 3.2, confusion. ADAMTS13 pending. Management?

Answer: Start plasma exchange immediately for presumed TTP. Don't wait for ADAMTS13.

Q9. Secondary HTN

45M with resistant HTN, K 2.9, HCO3 32. Most likely cause?

Answer: Primary aldosteronism (resistant HTN + hypokalemia + metabolic alkalosis)

Q10. Dialysis

Patient with ESRD, pericardial friction rub, BUN 180. Indication for dialysis?

Answer: Uremic pericarditis â€” emergent dialysis indication

Q11. Atheroembolic

70M with AKI, livedo reticularis, eosinophilia 2 days after cardiac cath. Diagnosis?

Answer: Atheroembolic disease (cholesterol emboli)

Q12. Anti-GBM

28M smoker with hemoptysis and RPGN. IF shows linear IgG. Treatment?

Answer: Plasmapheresis + cyclophosphamide + steroids for anti-GBM disease

Q13. SGLT2i

Patient with T2DM and CKD (eGFR 35, UACR 450) on max ACEi. What to add for renoprotection?

Answer: SGLT2 inhibitor (proven benefit in CREDENCE, DAPA-CKD)

Q14. Acid-Base

pH 7.28, pCO2 24, HCO3 11, AG 22. What's the primary disorder and is compensation appropriate?

Answer: AGMA. Expected pCO2 = 1.5(11)+8 = 24.5. Compensation appropriate.

Q15. Contrast

Patient with CKD (GFR 40) needs CT with contrast. Prevention strategy?

Answer: IV isotonic saline before and after. NAC and bicarb have no proven benefit (PRESERVE).
